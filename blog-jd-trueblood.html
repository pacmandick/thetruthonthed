I Reached Out to Dallas Voice — and Recalled My Encounter with Their Contributor, JD Trueblood

Posted: August 1, 2025
Category: Blog

Last week, I reached out to Dallas Voice. I sent them a straightforward, factual pitch — an investigative story based on my experience being digitally stalked, impersonated, harassed, and sabotaged in Dallas. I mentioned the police reports. I gave them my DOJ Civil Rights Complaint ID (606262-MPH). I offered to share screenshots, evidence, and the names of those involved.

Their response?

“You need to speak with an attorney and law enforcement before speaking with the press.”

It was a polite dismissal. But in reading that email, I was immediately reminded of something that happened last year — and ironically, it involved one of their own contributors: JD Trueblood.

We’ve never met in person. I only know him because he contacted me through a hookup app using a VOIP number. At first, it was the usual: some light flirting, wanting to PnP, sending a number. But then the tone shifted.

He began referencing parts of my life no stranger would know:

  • Old Facebook posts
  • Email handles I haven’t used in years
  • Reviews I had written, years ago, on public platforms

I traced the number he used. It was a dummy line — one of those free VOIP throwaways. So I called it.

It rang through to a real phone number — and the voicemail message gave it away.

I looked it up. It belonged to JD Trueblood.

I sent him a message at that real number. He casually responded and asked if I wanted to grab a drink, like the whole thing never happened.

He knew exactly what he was doing. And when caught, he just… moved on.

So when Dallas Voice responded with that deflection, telling me they couldn’t publish my story, I couldn’t help but think back to JD — a contributor to their own paper — who had trolled me for sport just a year earlier.

This is how they operate:

  • Act like they don’t see it.
  • Ignore the people who come forward.
  • Stay silent when their own are involved.

That’s why I created TruthOnTheD.com.
Because if I don’t tell these stories, no one will.
