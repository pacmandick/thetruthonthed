It’s Not Your Imagination — Bluetooth + Wi-Fi Are All They Need

It wasn’t the modem. It was proximity. And after that? Any connection would do.

I was told to chase the wrong problem — by people who acted like they were helping.

Ricky warned me about Cannon. Said he had hacked their phones, email, social, Wi-Fi — and the solution? “Just replace your modem.” I did. Twice. Didn’t change a thing.

Another guy showed up at my place and made comments that made it clear he knew what I was doing on my machine. He mocked the firewall, the router, the reformatting — said none of it mattered. And he was right. Because the modem wasn’t the problem. The proximity was.

🔍 The Real Attack Vector: Proximity-Based Exploits

This is what they use:

  • Bluebugging – remote control via Bluetooth
  • Bluesnarfing – stealing messages, files, contacts
  • KARMA attacks – spoofing trusted Wi-Fi names to auto-connect devices
  • Evil Twin APs – faking legit Wi-Fi to intercept everything

You don’t need to open an attachment. You don’t even need to click. All it takes is Bluetooth or Wi-Fi enabled, and your device trying to reconnect to something it *thinks* is safe.

💡 It’s Not About the Internet

The modem was never the issue. Once they’re in — *any* connection becomes the tool. Wi-Fi, cellular, tethered, doesn’t matter. The initial exploit happens through proximity. Everything else is just the channel they ride afterward.

🍏 What Apple Won’t Tell You

Apple knows Bluetooth is insecure. But they can’t patch around it without breaking their ecosystem — AirDrop, Handoff, AirPods, etc. So instead? They downplay. They minimize. They ignore. When I reported what happened, they sent me home with a factory reset and a “have a nice day.”

🛑 This Is By Design

The goal is to waste your time. Make you reset things that don’t need resetting. Make you feel crazy. Make you give up. But now that I understand the pattern? I see it for what it is — and I’m not chasing the wrong enemy anymore.
