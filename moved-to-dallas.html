Here Comes the Lockdown, Welcome to Dallas

⸻

I packed my car, left behind everything I knew, and landed in Dallas the day before the world shut down. March 2020. No job lined up, no friends waiting for me — just instinct and the belief that this city could be a new start.

I woke up the next morning and the city was closed. Lockdown. Quarantine. Stay inside. Don’t breathe near anyone. I barely had time to figure out how to get to the grocery store before people started panic-buying toilet paper.

You’d think that would’ve been a sign.

But here’s the thing — the part no one talks about — Dallas didn’t really stop. The city put on a show for the cameras, but behind closed doors, certain circles kept spinning. The parties never stopped. The scene never shut down. If anything, it got darker, louder, more intense.

And yeah, I thought I could handle it. I told myself I had control, that I was just blowing off steam like everyone else. But looking back, I was chasing something. Connection. Escape. Maybe just a feeling of being seen.

What I found instead was a fast-moving current I wasn’t ready for. One that looked like fun on the surface but pulled people under when no one was watching.

I didn’t know it then, but that move — that one choice — would set off a chain of events that I’m still unpacking to this day.

This is where it started. Not the glamorous version. Not the version curated for likes or status. The real one. The version that forces you to choose: disappear quietly… or survive loudly.
